The University of British Columbia and Simon Fraser University Sikh Students' Associations cordially invite the BC Sikh community to Stand Up and Take Action.

Sikhya - The School of Learning

Sikhya - The School of Learning is a state of the art educational institution that is dedicated to providing high quality education exclusively to students of the underprivileged class. Sikhya's mission is to educate the most underprivileged of children in order to give them knowledge, skills, values, love, food and clothes, the power to earn, and most importantly a bright future and a chance - without any fees or any compromise.

After opening its doors in February 2006, this institution has grown over to 800 students. Sikhya's modern, 50,000 square-foot campus houses state-of-the-art infrastructure, including computers, modern sports facilities and multi-media teaching facilities. Its complete academic course of study covers 14 years of education, going up from nursery to class 12.

This community service requires an extensive amount of resources and can only continue with our community's generous donations. In an effort to provide some monetary assistance, partial proceeds of Nihaal 2010 will be donated to Guru Nanak Vidya Bhandar Trust, which runs several schools and orphanages, including Sikhya.

If you are interested in volunteering your time, effort, money, and ideas, contact Sikhya directly.

As Sikhya has determined to Stand Up and Take Action to help the less fortunate and assist those in need, the SFU and UBC SSAs hope to inspire our peers to take part in similar worthy causes.

For more information, visit Sikhya or watch a video tour of Sikhya.
